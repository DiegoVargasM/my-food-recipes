Here you will learn how to make the perfect Aji de Gallina

Recipe description

Ají de gallina is a Peruvian chicken stew. The dish is considered a popular Peruvian comfort food, and the name translates to "chicken chili" or "hen's chili" in English.

Ají de gallina is composed of a sofrito base made by sautéing red onion, garlic, and ají amarillo together, and adding shredded poached poultry and stock.

The stew is then thickened with bread soaked in milk or evaporated milk, cheese such as parmesan, and ground nuts such as pecans or walnuts.

Ingredients

  • A chicken breast
  • a bay leaf
  • 2 cups of mixed vegetables (carrot, celery and leek)
  • a french bread
  • 2 cups of milk
  • ¼ cup of oil
  • a chopped onion
  • 4 minced garlic cloves
  • ½ cup of yellow pepper cream
  • ¼ teaspoon cumin
  • ½ teaspoon toothpick
  • ¼ teaspoon cumin
  • ¼ cup toasted and chopped pecans
  • ½ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 4 yellow sliced and cooked potatoes
  • 4 black botija olives
  • 2 boiled eggs
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Cook the breast with salt, laurel and vegetables, separate the broth and fray the hen. Then, crumble the bread and let it soak for a few minutes in a cup of milk, to finally blend it. Reserve the preparation.
  2. Separately, in a pot, prepare the dressing with five tablespoons of oil, onion, garlic cloves, yellow chili cream, cumin and toothpick. Sauté small amounts of the chicken broth in the dressing and add the blended bread. Cook, stirring constantly, adding the broth.
  3. Season, add the remaining milk and cook over low heat. Incorporate the frayed chicken, the pecans, and spread the Parmesan cheese. Wait for it to take point.
